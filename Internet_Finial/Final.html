Bryce Canyon

Bryce Canyon History

Bryce Canyon National Park's history spans from ancient Native American occupation to its designation as a national park. Archaeological evidence suggests human presence in the area for at least 10,000 years, with various cultures inhabiting the region. The park's name is derived from Ebenezer Bryce, a Mormon pioneer who settled in the area in the 1870s. Preservation efforts led to Bryce Canyon being declared a National Monument in 1923 and a National Park in 1928.

    Why To Visit

    Bryce Canyon National Park is a must-visit destination for several reasons, including its unique natural rock formations, breathtaking views, and opportunities for outdoor activities like hiking and stargazing. The park also offers a chance to experience a peaceful escape from crowds and enjoy the natural beauty of the area. It's unique formations create an otherworldly landscape that is both awe-inspiring and photogenic. In the winter people who visit can a cross-country ski, making it a unique winter destination.
